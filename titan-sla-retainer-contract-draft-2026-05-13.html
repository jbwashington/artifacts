This Master Services Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on effective_date between:

titan_legal_name ("Provider"), a titan_entity_type with its principal place of business at titan_address; and

client_legal_name ("Client"), with its principal place of business at client_address.

Provider and Client are each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties."

1. Services

Provider will furnish software development and technical support services ("Services") to Client on an ongoing basis. The general scope is described in the Project Requirements Document ("PRD") referenced at prd_reference, which is incorporated by reference. The PRD may be updated by mutual written agreement.

Services may include, without limitation:

  • Software development and feature implementation
  • Bug fixes and maintenance
  • Technical advisory and consultation
  • Response to Client's support requests within the timeframes set in §4

2. Term

This Agreement is effective on effective_date and continues on a month-to-month basis (each month a "Retainer Period") until terminated under §11. The first Retainer Period begins on term_start.

Each Retainer Period automatically renews unless either Party gives at least notice_period_days days' written notice prior to the end of the then-current Retainer Period.

3. Fees and Payment

3.1 Monthly Retainer

Client will pay Provider $monthly_retainer_amount USD per month (the "Monthly Retainer"), payable on the same day of each month as the Effective Date. Payment is made via Stripe automatic billing on the payment method Client maintains on file with Provider.

3.2 Included Active Hours

The Monthly Retainer includes up to hours_included Active Hours of Services per Retainer Period.

"Active Hours" means the time during which Provider's development tools — including AI-assisted development environments — are actively executing work on Client's project, measured by automated logging in Provider's systems. Time spent on idle processes, deployments, queue waits, or asynchronous background tasks that do not require active attention is not counted.

3.3 Carryover of Unused Hours

Up to carryover_percent% of unused Active Hours in a Retainer Period roll over to the next Retainer Period, capped at carryover_cap_months Retainer Period(s) of carryover. Carried-over hours that exceed the cap expire.

3.4 Overage Rate

Services performed in excess of the included Active Hours (after applying any carryover) are billed at a tiered hourly rate based on the nature of the work:

TierRate (USD/hr)Examples
Junior$rate_juniorRoutine maintenance, content updates, small bug fixes
Standard$rate_standardFeature development, integration work
Senior$rate_seniorArchitecture, complex debugging, production incidents
Strategic$rate_strategicSystem design, planning, migration leadership

Provider determines the appropriate tier for each unit of overage work and shows the assignment on each invoice. Client may request a written justification for any tier assignment.

3.5 Overage Approval Threshold

Provider will obtain Client's written approval (email is sufficient) before incurring more than $overage_approval_threshold USD of overage charges in a single Retainer Period, except where immediate action is required to address a Critical Issue under §4.1.

3.6 Out-of-Scope Projects

Substantial new features, major redesigns, or work outside ordinary maintenance and iteration ("Out-of-Scope Projects") will be quoted separately as fixed-scope engagements with their own statements of work. Out-of-Scope work is not billed against included Active Hours or overage rates.

3.7 Invoices and Payment Terms

Provider will issue an invoice on the first business day of each Retainer Period covering the upcoming Monthly Retainer and the prior period's overage charges (if any). Invoices are due net 15 days from issue. Late payments accrue interest at 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower.

3.8 Failed Payments

If automatic payment fails, Client has 10 business days to cure before Provider may suspend Services. Provider will notify Client in writing of any failed payment within two business days of the failure.

4. Response Service Levels

4.1 Critical Issues

A "Critical Issue" means a system Client uses in active business operation is down, broken, or producing materially incorrect data. For Critical Issues, Provider will acknowledge the issue within response_sla_critical and begin substantive work within the same window.

4.2 Standard Requests

For all other Services requests, Provider will acknowledge within response_sla_standard business days. "Business day" means Monday through Friday, excluding U.S. federal holidays, between 9:00 AM and 6:00 PM Eastern Time.

4.3 Out-of-Hours Work

Work performed outside business hours at Client's request is billed at 1.5× the applicable tier rate. Provider may decline non-critical out-of-hours requests.

5. Client Responsibilities

Client agrees to:

  • Provide Provider with reasonable access to systems, accounts, repositories, and information necessary to perform Services
  • Respond to Provider's requests for input, feedback, or approvals within five business days
  • Maintain a valid payment method on file
  • Designate at least one Authorized Representative empowered to approve overage charges, scope changes, and PRD amendments

Provider is not responsible for delays caused by Client's failure to meet these responsibilities and may extend any deadlines accordingly.

6. Intellectual Property

6.1 Work Product

Upon Client's full payment for the Services in which a Work Product was created, Provider assigns to Client all right, title, and interest in custom software, written materials, designs, configurations, and other deliverables developed specifically for Client ("Work Product").

6.2 Pre-existing Materials

Provider retains all rights to its pre-existing tools, frameworks, libraries, methodologies, templates, and general know-how used in performing the Services ("Pre-existing Materials"). Provider grants Client a perpetual, non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use Pre-existing Materials solely as embedded in the Work Product.

6.3 Third-Party Materials

Work Product may incorporate third-party open-source or commercial components. Such components remain governed by their respective licenses; Provider will identify any non-permissive licenses on request.

6.4 Portfolio Rights

Provider may identify Client as a client of Provider and may display a general description of the Work Product in Provider's portfolio and marketing materials, provided no Confidential Information is disclosed.

7. Confidentiality

Each Party may receive information from the other that is marked confidential, or that a reasonable person would understand to be confidential ("Confidential Information").

The receiving Party will:

  • use Confidential Information only to perform under this Agreement;
  • protect it with at least the same care it uses for its own confidential information, and in no event less than reasonable care; and
  • not disclose it to any third party without the disclosing Party's prior written consent.

Confidentiality obligations survive termination for three (3) years.

Confidential Information does not include information that (a) is or becomes public through no fault of the receiving Party, (b) was lawfully known to the receiving Party prior to disclosure, (c) is rightfully obtained from a third party without a duty of confidentiality, or (d) is independently developed without use of the disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

8. Warranties and Disclaimer

Provider warrants that Services will be performed in a professional and workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards.

EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY PROVIDED IN THIS AGREEMENT, SERVICES AND WORK PRODUCT ARE PROVIDED "AS IS" WITHOUT WARRANTIES OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING ANY WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE, AND NON-INFRINGEMENT. Provider does not warrant that Services or Work Product will be uninterrupted, error-free, or compatible with any specific third-party system unless expressly agreed in writing.

9. Limitation of Liability

EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY (§7) OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS (§10), NEITHER PARTY WILL BE LIABLE TO THE OTHER FOR ANY INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, INCLUDING LOST PROFITS OR LOST DATA, EVEN IF ADVISED OF THE POSSIBILITY OF SUCH DAMAGES.

EACH PARTY'S TOTAL CUMULATIVE LIABILITY UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IS LIMITED TO THE AMOUNTS PAID OR PAYABLE BY CLIENT TO PROVIDER IN THE TWELVE (12) MONTHS IMMEDIATELY PRECEDING THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO THE CLAIM.

10. Indemnification

Each Party will defend and indemnify the other Party against third-party claims, damages, and reasonable attorneys' fees arising from the indemnifying Party's:

  • gross negligence or willful misconduct;
  • material breach of this Agreement; or
  • in Provider's case, infringement of a third party's intellectual property rights by Work Product (excluding any infringement attributable to Client's instructions, Client-supplied materials, or modifications made by Client).

Indemnification is conditioned on prompt written notice and reasonable cooperation by the indemnified Party.

11. Termination

11.1 Termination for Convenience

Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time with at least notice_period_days days' written notice. Client remains obligated for the Monthly Retainer through the end of the Retainer Period in which termination becomes effective.

11.2 Termination for Cause

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party materially breaches the Agreement and fails to cure the breach within cure_period_days days after receipt of written notice describing the breach in reasonable detail.

11.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

  • Provider will deliver to Client all Work Product for which Client has paid in full;
  • Client will pay all outstanding invoices, including pro-rated overage for the final Retainer Period;
  • Each Party will return or destroy the other Party's Confidential Information on request.

Provisions that by their nature should survive — including §6 (IP), §7 (Confidentiality), §8 (Disclaimer), §9 (Liability), §10 (Indemnification), and §12 (Miscellaneous) — survive termination.

12. Miscellaneous

12.1 Independent Contractor

Provider is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, employer-employee relationship, or agency. Provider is responsible for its own taxes, insurance, and benefits.

12.2 Assignment

Neither Party may assign this Agreement or any rights under it without the other Party's prior written consent, except in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets.

12.3 Notices

Notices under this Agreement must be in writing and delivered by email to the addresses in the signature block. Notices of termination, breach, or indemnification must additionally be sent by certified mail or nationally recognized overnight courier.

12.4 Governing Law and Venue

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of governing_state, without regard to its conflicts of law principles. Disputes arising out of or relating to this Agreement will be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts located in governing_county, governing_state, and each Party consents to personal jurisdiction in those courts.

12.5 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with any PRD or statements of work expressly incorporated by reference, is the entire agreement between the Parties on its subject matter and supersedes any prior oral or written agreements on that subject matter. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both Parties.

12.6 Severability and Waiver

If any provision of this Agreement is held unenforceable, the remainder of the Agreement remains in full force and effect. No waiver of any provision is effective unless in writing and signed by the waiving Party.

12.7 Counterparts and Electronic Signatures

This Agreement may be signed in counterparts, including by electronic signature or PDF, each of which is deemed an original and which together constitute one instrument.

12.8 Force Majeure

Neither Party is liable for delays or failures in performance caused by events beyond its reasonable control, including natural disasters, war, civil unrest, government actions, internet or cloud-service outages of significant scope, and pandemics. The affected Party must promptly notify the other and resume performance as soon as reasonably practicable.
